Project Overview

The Nyakuza Bridge Project is a 128-meter pedestrian suspension bridge spanning the Ingwavuma River, connecting the communities of Emanyonyaneni and Nsizatje in Hosea Inkhundla, Shiselweni Region. It is currently the longest pedestrian bridge in Eswatini.

Our team partnered with Engineers in Action, local masons, and community members to design and construct the bridge, improving year-round access to critical infrastructure and enhancing safety, education, and economic opportunity. Over the course of a year, we developed engineering plans before traveling on-site for construction. Our work on-site covered every step of the construction process—excavating, mixing concrete, building masonry walls, setting cable sag, and decking—always with the help of dedicated community members for whom the bridge will be most impactful.

Alongside supporting design, planning , and construction, I spearheaded project documentation and media efforts to create comprehensive technical resources and educational videos for future international teams.
